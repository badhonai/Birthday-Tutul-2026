# 🎂 Birthday Card Website — A Late but Loving Delivery

> *Started at 11:00 PM. Meant to send at 12:00 AM. Actually sent at 2:44 AM the same day. Worth every sleepless minute.*

---

## 💌 About This Project

This is a handcrafted birthday card website, delivered as a **special HTML email** — not a template, not a generic e-card, but something built from scratch with genuine effort and a lot of late-night caffeine.

The goal was simple: make someone feel truly celebrated on their birthday, not just remembered. So instead of a text message or a store-bought card, this project became a full custom web experience packed into a single email.

---

## ⏰ The Honest Timeline

| Time | What Happened |
|------|--------------|
| **11:00 PM** | Project started. "This won't take long," I said. |
| **12:00 AM** | The original send deadline. Missed it. |
| **Sometime after midnight** | Still coding. Still going. |
| **2:42 AM** | Finally finished. 3 hours and 42 minutes after starting. |
| **2:44 AM** | Email sent. Two minutes to compose myself and hit send. |

It was supposed to be a midnight surprise. It became an early-morning one. But it was done *right*, and that's what matters.

## 💭 Why It Was Late

Because making something meaningful takes time. The midnight deadline was the *ideal* — a birthday surprise right at the stroke of the day. But the reality of building something with real care, real polish, and real heart is that it doesn't always fit neatly into a schedule.

Every extra hour past midnight was a choice to do it properly instead of rushing it out the door.

---

## 🎁 What This Project Means

This wasn't a professional project. There's no client, no brief, no deadline set by anyone else. The deadline was self-imposed because the person receiving this deserved to wake up to something special.

They still got something special — it just arrived while they were awake.
